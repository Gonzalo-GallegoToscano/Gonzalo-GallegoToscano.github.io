About

I recently completed a BSc in Statistics, Economics and Finance at UCL, and this year I am starting an MSc in Statistics, with a focus on Statistical Finance, at Imperial College London.

My interests sit on the quantitative side of finance and the statistics that support it. I enjoy probability and statistical modelling, and the ways these ideas are used to understand markets, measure risk and reason under uncertainty. My degree brought together statistics and probability with economics and finance, which is the mix I want to keep building on.

Alongside my studies I have interned in investment banking, private equity and data analytics, which gave me exposure to credit, real estate and working with real financial data. Outside academics I cofounded a small charity and stay involved in student societies and football.

Projects

One week Value at Risk on the DAX and SPX
2025/26 · R, ARMA and GARCH, copulas

Estimated the one week Value at Risk of a DAX and SPX equity portfolio. Fitted ARMA and GARCH models to capture volatility clustering and conditional dynamics, applied the probability integral transform, and chose a copula to model the dependence between the two indices, then backtested the resulting estimates.

Predicting NBA field goals with Bayesian logistic regression
2025/26 · R, Bayesian inference, MCMC

Modelled whether an NBA shot is made, using Bayesian logistic regression. Implemented the estimation from scratch with a Metropolis Hastings sampler, chose priors, summarised the posterior through trace plots and summaries, and compared the model against a standard logistic regression using cross validation.

Modelling the 2016 EU Referendum
2024/25 · R, regression, clustering, PCA

Built a regression model for the share of Leave votes across 1,070 UK wards using around 49 census covariates. Applied hierarchical clustering to group regions and principal component analysis to handle correlation among ethnicity variables, then explored interactions to reach a parsimonious final model.
